Task 2: Analyze a Phishing Email Sample

1. Sample Phishing Email

Subject: Action Required: Update Your VPN Credentials Immediately

From: it-support@yourcompany.verifyportal.com

Dear Employee,

Our records indicate that your VPN credentials are due for a mandatory security update. Failure to update your credentials within 24 hours will result in restricted access to corporate resources.

Click here to update your credentials: https://vpn-yourcompany.com/update

For security reasons, this link will expire in 24 hours.

Thank you,
IT Support Team
YourCompany

2. Examine Sender's Email Address for Spoofing

Spoofing Indicator: The sender's domain (yourcompany.verifyportal.com) is not the legitimate domain of the organization (e.g., @yourcompany.com).

Tactic: Attackers often use subdomains or slight misspellings to mimic legitimate addresses.

3. Check Email Headers for Discrepancies

(Hypothetical analysis using an online header analyzer like MXToolbox)

Discrepancies Found:

  • From: field shows it-support@yourcompany.verifyportal.com, but the Return-Path: may point to a different domain (e.g., bounce@malicious-server.com).
  • Originating IP address may trace to an unrelated location (e.g., a cloud server in a foreign country).

4. Identify Suspicious Links or Attachments

Suspicious Link:

  • Displayed URL: https://vpn-yourcompany.com/update
  • Actual URL (on hover): https://malicious-site.com/phish-vpn
  • Red Flag: Mismatch between displayed and actual URLs.

Attachment Analysis: None in this sample, but real phishing emails might include malicious files like VPN_Update.exe or Credentials_Form.pdf.

5. Urgent or Threatening Language

Examples in Email:

  • "Failure to update your credentials within 24 hours will result in restricted access."
  • "For security reasons, this link will expire in 24 hours."

Goal: Creates panic to bypass rational scrutiny.

6. Mismatched URLs

Technique: Hyperlinks disguise malicious destinations.

Example: The link text (vpn-yourcompany.com) appears legitimate but redirects to malicious-site.com.

7. Verify Presence of Spelling or Grammar Errors

In This Sample: None (well-crafted phishing emails may avoid errors).

Common Errors in Real Attacks:

  • Misspellings (e.g., "spe ling" instead of "spelling").
  • Awkward phrasing (e.g., "Kindly to update your account").

8. Summary of Phishing Traits

  • Spoofed Sender: Fake domain (verifyportal.com).
  • Urgent Language: Threats of account suspension.
  • Mismatched URLs: Hidden malicious link.
  • Header Discrepancies: Inconsistent From and Return-Path.
  • Lack of Personalization: Generic greeting ("Dear Employee").
